This poem reflects a person's optimistic spirit for life and boundless creativity even when facing hardship. His imaginations of cloud reflections as boats berthing the setting sun, and waves forming stairs to the heavens, are far different than the reality of his journey of crossing a winter sea. Potentially, he might be stuck with nothing, but he still can sing about the moon, play strings formed by vapor and pour wind as wine. I wrote this poem to remind myself of keeping a good state of mind when facing difficult research problems.

Robot arm cutting cake

What can we do for someone's birthday with a Kinova robot arm? After our lab agreed to do a robot-cake-cutting video, I came up with the idea of using my old spatula, which has an handle that fits almost perfectly inside the robot's gripper, and a blade thin enough to slice through layers of cake and fillings. Most importantly, cutting with a spatula instead of a knife avoids colliding the gripper with the table and accidental bloodshed. We guided the robot like a pupet over a designed trajectory. The path was recorded and replayed over a real cake. The last slice was unplanned.
